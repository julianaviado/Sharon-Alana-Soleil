If overthinking, overgiving, overachieving, or overextending have become your default way of moving through life, you may be experiencing what I call…

noun

The internal pressure to do more, give more, and expect more of yourself until you lose sight of what you need and what matters most.

Meet Sharon

Life has a way of revealing what matters most.

At 26, a sudden health crisis left Sharon in a wheelchair, facing the possibility that she might never walk again.

Some of life’s greatest lessons come from the challenging experiences we’d never choose. That season taught Sharon something she has never forgotten: lasting change doesn’t come from trying harder. It begins by understanding the patterns that have been shaping our lives.

Today, that belief is the foundation of the Freedom to Flow Method™, which Sharon shares through her speaking and coaching to help the people others count on move beyond pressure and become more present for what matters most.

Answers to questions

Before you book

Is this therapy?
No. Coaching is not therapy or medical care, and it doesn’t replace either. Coaching works forward from where you are. Therapy treats a condition. If what you bring me needs a clinician, I’ll say so in the first conversation and give you names.
I don’t have time for this. That’s the whole problem.
I know. That objection is the pattern talking, and it’s the reason the work is scoped the way it is. The Power of Your Presence is three hours, once. Not a standing commitment, not homework. If three hours is genuinely impossible, that’s worth saying out loud on the call, because it tells us both something.
Where do sessions actually happen?
Remotely, wherever you are. The Power of Your Presence, Your Present Paths, and The Real MVP all run over video, so where you live doesn’t decide whether we can work together. The only exception is Go With Your Flow Privé, which goes wherever you choose.
How long before anything actually changes?
Most people notice the pattern inside the first session, because naming it is most of it. Changing what you do about it takes longer, usually six to eight weeks of small experiments. Anyone promising faster is selling relief, not change.
Do I need to know what I want first?
No. Most people arrive knowing only that something is off. That’s a fine place to start. Finding the shape of it is the work, not the prerequisite for the work.
What actually happens on a What Matters Most Call?
Thirty minutes. You talk about where you are and what’s going on. I listen and ask. At the end we decide together whether this is aligned for both of us. If I’m not the right person, I’ll tell you who is.
